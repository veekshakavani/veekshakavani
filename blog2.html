Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the Indian state of Karnataka. It is an ancient city that was once the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, which ruled southern India from the 14th to the 16th centuries. Hampi is known for its magnificent ruins, including temples, palaces, and other structures that provide a glimpse into the city's glorious past.

Some of the notable attractions in Hampi include the Virupaksha Temple, which is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is one of the oldest functioning temples in India; the Vittala Temple, which is known for its intricate carvings and the famous musical pillars that produce different sounds when tapped; the Hampi Bazaar, which was once a bustling market street; and the Lotus Mahal, which was a royal palace.

Apart from the historical attractions, Hampi is also famous for its natural beauty. The city is situated on the banks of the Tungabhadra River, and there are several hills and valleys that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
